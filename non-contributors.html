Last year, while living in Miracle Mile, I started taking walks with my roommate and recording them with a GPS tracking service. I eventually requested my data from this service and sent the coordinates to the Google Street View API, returning a set of images that traced my paths through the neighborhood. I was really affected by the presence of the Google logo on this landscape and how the Street View images superimposed public and branded space.

This narrative of commerce is reinscribed into the neighborhood through its status as a Historic Preservation Overlay Zone (HPOZ), a zoning designation that codifies an area's historical significance through its architecture, natural features, and public infrastructure. Non-Contributors attempts to surface that narrative by captioning the Street View images with text from a modified form of the HPOZ planning document, where I searched for the features of Miracle Mile that signified the area's value, and removed them. The captions are computationally generated from the redacted text; the title references a committee-approved label assigned to a feature or site in the neighborhood that has been irreversibly altered or was built outside of a specified period of relevance, and thus is not included in the HPOZ.
